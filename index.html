Answer templates

Reusable answer shape (for messy prompts)

  1. Restate the problem in one sentence.
  2. Clarify: “I’d meet with SMEs to gather more info, but I’d rather act with ~80% than wait for 100% and miss timing.”
  3. Apply a strategy (0→100 / QA / Buy-in / Prioritize).
  4. Execute: owners, cadence, artifacts, risk log, milestones.
  5. Optional: a slice of a relevant anecdote + measurable result.
Case structuring snippet (ARR lever framing)

SaaS ARR equation framing:

Ending ARR = Beginning ARR + New ARR + Expanded ARR − Churned ARR − Contracted ARR

  • Increase New ARR: grow top-of-funnel, win rate, ACV
  • Increase Expanded ARR: upsell/cross-sell, reduce contraction
  • Decrease Churned ARR: support, user research, ease-of-use

Close with: “I’d dig into which lever has the biggest impact and prioritize from there.”

Core strategies

Your core playbook (fast recall)

0 → 100 (Scope → Plan → Execute → Scale)

Scoping / Discovery (5 questions)

  • Where are we?
  • Where do we want to go?
  • Why do we want to go there?
  • Who do we need to consult?
  • What data do we have to inform us?

Planning

  • How do we get there? people, resources, money, time, target audience
  • How do we measure success? financial, efficiency, operational, quality

Execution

  • Kickoff: key contacts, vision, process, next steps, FAQs
  • Prioritize / resolve conflict: empathize → OKRs → data tradeoffs → importance vs urgency → “downhill 1-way door” checkpoints
  • Accountability / QA: reporting cadence → reflect/correct/reward
  • Scale: documentation, empower people to teach, adjacent impact
Buy-in (Data + E-E-E)
  • Empathize: understand their needs and why THEY should care
  • Equip: guidelines, visibility trackers, feedback loops
  • Empower: affirm strengths, create safe space for growth
  • Data: show tradeoffs; use what you have; move with imperfect info
Root cause analysis (bias to action)
  • Better to act with ~80% than freeze for 100%
  • Bias toward myself (what can I do right now?)
  • Who / what / when: isolate the failure mode
Comms (tech vs non-tech)
  • Tech: dashboard + raw data access
  • Non-tech: impact framing (%) + concise summaries, no jargon
Quality assurance (manual + automated)
  • Write clear guidelines with SMEs; coordinate training
  • Manual: SME reviews samples
  • Automated: error dashboards + audits
  • Feedback loop + incentives

If quality drops: root cause → fix guidelines → retrain high-risk tasks/edge cases → reinforce feedback loop + incentives

Speed + accuracy: automate routing by expertise; QA high-impact tasks; incentives require both

Metrics

Scorecard cheat sheet

30/60/90 OKRs (examples)
  • O: Seamless people integration with 0 pay disruption
    • KR: 100% on-time W2 payroll / 1099 payouts first 2 cycles
  • O: Compliance up to standard
    • KR: 100% anti-harassment training by Day 45
    • KR: 95% other compliance by Day 30
  • O: Staff adoption of platform
    • KR: 80% CRM active users by Day 60
    • KR: 90% workflow error-free audits
Financial metrics
  • MoM change in ARR, topline revenue, net income
  • New ARR, Expanded ARR, Churned ARR, Contracted ARR
  • Synergy realized vs planned
  • Actual integration spend vs budget
  • Top 50 account retention

Phases

5 phases (clean timeline narrative)

1) Plan / Prep (start ~3 months prior)
  • Establish core functional leads
  • Create integration project plans
  • Draft ROEs
2) Discovery (start ~2 months prior → finish by close)
  • Introduce core + AcquiredCo functional leads
  • DD deep dive Qs; use org chart to root out synergies
3) Functional Integration (start ~1 month prior → ongoing)
  • Earnout milestones + check-in cadence
  • Functional alignment + synergy decisions
  • Draft employee-facing FAQs
  • Day 1 welcome presentation + email FAQs
  • 1:1 / focus groups (guidelines, ROEs, role expectations, training)
  • 30 + 90 day surveys; review results
  • Establish dashboard cadence
4) Systems Integration (prep ~1 month prior → finish ~6–9 months post-close)
  • Draft customer auto-reply email + website FAQs; Day 1 publish
  • Provide software + hardware access
  • Track headcount/vendor synergies; sunset tools
  • HRIS migration
  • ERP (finance) migration
  • CRM migration: account mapping, SKU mapping, contracts, contacts, tickets
  • Lead-to-cash consolidation: lead→opp (marketing), opp→quote (sales), quote→order (legal), order→cash (finance), cash→delivery (support/services)
5) Product Integration (6–12+ months post-close)
  • Product cycle consolidation
  • R&D process consolidation (often overlaps systems/product)

Reverse interview

Questions to ask (keep them talking)

  1. What’s your decision timeline?
  2. What makes this role / company stand out versus others you’re considering?
  3. Why are you hiring for this position now — and is there budget for multiple integration hires?
  4. Are acquisitions part of long-term strategy or a short-term play to add ARR / put out fires?
  5. What’s the biggest headache you want this role to solve? (3 wishes?)
  6. Anything unique about your integration strategy I should know? (acq/year; pain points; retention tactics)
  7. How is success defined here (metrics + soft skills)?
  8. How often do roles get rescoped mid-process — and what prevents that?

Why AEC firms acquire (general)

  2. Client acquisition (project-focused)
  3. Geographic expansion
  4. Business line diversification (offset sector downturns)
  5. Inorganic $ growth
Plain-English geospatial explanation

Geospatial/survey/mapping is “measuring the real world” — where things are, elevation, and change — so teams can plan/build accurately.

  • Imagery: high-res photos from planes/drones/satellites
  • LiDAR: “laser tape measure” scanning ground/buildings/trees for precise 3D maps
  • Used for road repairs, utility vegetation risk, flood maps, construction accuracy
